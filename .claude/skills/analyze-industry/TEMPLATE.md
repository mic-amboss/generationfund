# [Industry Name] — Industry Deep Dive

---

## Thesis

One sentence. Falsifiable. State the verdict and the structural reason.

> *Format: "[Industry] is a **[compounder factory / selective compounder / mostly value-traps / avoid]** because [structural reason], unless [concrete falsifier]."*

> *Example: "Vertical-market software is a **compounder factory** because high switching costs + recurring revenue + fragmented end-markets + capital-light economics combine to support repeatable M&A at high incremental ROIC, unless AI-native rebuilds erode the switching-cost advantage faster than incumbents can ship AI features."*

---

## Industry Overview

A concise portrait in 2-3 paragraphs. What is the industry, how big is it, who buys/sells what, and why does it deserve a quality-compounder lens?

Define the boundary precisely. State whether the *whole industry* or a *specific profit pool within it* is the unit of analysis. (Example: "specialty E&S commercial insurance," not "insurance.")

End with one paragraph: what makes this industry *interesting now* (a structural shift, an underappreciated tailwind, a wave of public listings).

## History & Evolution

The industry's story as a narrative, not a timeline. Focus on the inflection points — formation, technology shifts, regulatory changes, structural consolidation events, crises that reshaped the players.

A reader should finish this section understanding *why the industry looks the way it does today* and *what shaped the current incumbents*.

3-5 paragraphs. Spend most ink on the last 20-30 years.

## Compounder Factory Verdict

The diagnostic synthesis. Apply each framework explicitly:

### Buffett binary classification
- **Capital intensity:** heavy / moderate / light → [score + evidence]
- **Pricing power:** weak / moderate / strong → [score + evidence]
- **Commodity nature:** high / moderate / low → [score + evidence]
- **Verdict:** pursue / selective / avoid

### BCG Advantage Matrix
- Volume / Specialization / Fragmented / Stalemate? [+ reasoning]

### Akre's reinvestment moat
- Can leading players redeploy capital at high rates of return? For how long?

### Sleep's Scale Economies Shared
- Is the dominant model designed to share scale with customers, creating a self-reinforcing flywheel?

### Smith's exclusion filter
- Structurally cyclical? Capital-heavy? Levered by default? — if yes to multiple, raise the bar significantly.

## Idiosyncratic Observations

The most valuable section. The non-obvious things you'd only learn by reading the primary sources carefully.

Examples of what belongs here:
- Industry-specific accounting peculiarities that obscure or reveal economics
- Cultural norms that affect compounding outcomes (family-business prevalence, founder tenure norms, etc.)
- Hidden value pools that aren't obvious from sector-level data
- Structural dynamics that go against conventional wisdom
- Quirks of how the industry sells, hires, regulates itself, or competes
- Forces that *appear* important but aren't (and vice versa)

Each item should be a mini-story: what it is, why it matters, what it tells you about the industry.
